Threat Types

What Bad Hosts
Do to You

CODE SNOOPING

Staff with server access can open and read every file in your hosted project — your logic, your API calls, your entire codebase — without your knowledge or permission.

TOKEN THEFT

Your Discord bot token is the master key to your bot. If it lives in your project files and the host can read them, your token is compromised. Full bot takeover is possible.

PUBLIC EXPOSURE

Private code posted publicly in support channels or Discord servers — used as "evidence" in disputes. Your IP and logic become public without consent.

EXTORTION BANS

Accounts suspended without warning, with reinstatement gated behind paid plan upgrades. Access to your own bot held hostage to force purchases.

SILENT MONITORING

No disclosure that staff can access files. No terms explaining what data is read or stored. You host your bot thinking it's private — it isn't.

NO LEGAL PROTECTION

Free-tier hosts often have no privacy policy, no GDPR compliance, and no accountability. You have zero legal recourse if your code or secrets are misused.

Defense Guide

How to Stay
Actually Safe

REGENERATE YOUR TOKEN NOW

Go to the Discord Developer Portal and regenerate your bot token immediately. Treat the old one as stolen.

NEVER HARDCODE SECRETS

Store your token in a .env file, load it with python-dotenv, and always add .env to .gitignore.

READ THE TERMS OF SERVICE

Before hosting anywhere, check their ToS for staff access clauses. If it's vague or missing, assume they can read everything.

CHOOSE VERIFIED SAFE HOSTS

Use providers with clear privacy policies, isolated environments, and a reputation the community trusts. See our safe hosts list.

REPORT BAD ACTORS

If a host violated your privacy, document it with screenshots and submit a report below. Protecting the community means speaking up.
